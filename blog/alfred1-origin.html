The Origin of Alfred 1

Posted: November 2025

I started Alfred 1 because I wanted a robot that could mimic real-world problems—not just a toy, not just a remote-controlled car, but a stepping stone from “robot dreamer” to “actual robotics engineer.”

Alfred wasn’t just a random name I picked. It’s a mashup of the robots and characters that shaped my imagination growing up:

  • Alfred — Batman’s butler
  • 5 — A nod to Johnny 5
  • Butler aesthetic — I always imagined him like a Minion in a butler outfit
  • Voice — Deep and slow like Lurch from The Addams Family

That’s the vision behind Alfred 5, the full-sized robotic butler I want to build someday. A robot that can answer the door, bring a drink, maybe vacuum…the “dream build.”

But reality has limits: I’m one guy with a full-time job, a house remodel in progress, and about ten side projects running through my brain at any given hour. If I jumped straight into the full-scale robot, I’d drown before I ever got a wheel turning.

Start with Alfred 1. Learn. Build. Fail. Improve. Repeat.

Alfred 1 uses a Raspberry Pi 5 with an SSD, a RPLidar A1M8, and smaller DC gear motors with encoders. Basically, all the core components of Alfred 5…but right-sized for a beginner who wants to actually finish a robot before he dies of old age.

And yes, the project’s physical state is pure chaos in my robotics lab. But in my mind? It’s systematically beautiful.

The first battle: ROS2 vs Ubuntu vs Cameras

ROS2 is the whole reason Alfred 1 exists. I want to learn it, use it, break it, rebuild it, and eventually master it.

But my first attempts… chef’s kiss of chaos:

  • Ubuntu 24.04 + ArduCam = Nope
  • Tried switching to Raspberry Pi OS = Nope × 10
  • ROS2 Jazzy said “Not today, buddy”
  • Back to Ubuntu
  • New USB camera
  • Finally…progress

Every robotics journey starts with something incredibly stupid causing three days of troubleshooting. Alfred 1 is no different.

What’s coming next

In the next post, I’ll go deeper into the bill of materials, the design philosophy, the software goals, and what Alfred 1’s purpose is beyond being a cool-looking robot with blinking lights.

If you haven’t fallen asleep yet… welcome to the start of something big.
